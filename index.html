NTU IoT Research Group

NTU IoT Research Group

We are a research group based in the School of Computer Science and Engineering, Nanyang Technological University. We focus on the research, design, and evaluation of networked, energy-efficient, and secure sensing systems found in the Internet of Things (IoT) and its AI-empowered generation (AIoT). Our research has two main sub-directions of IoT sensing systems/applications and security/privacy of AIoT sensing. In the first sub-direction, with a strong experimental focus, we study a number of sensing modalities (e.g., powerline radiation, radio frequency, acoustics, image, thermal, and energy), exploit them to construct system functions and applications. In the second sub-direction, we study the security and privacy of AIoT sensing systems that use machine learning to process the sensed data. Due to the immediate application potential, our research has been funded externally by government authorities and companies in the ICT, energy, and manufacturing sectors.

Acoustic and imagery sensing

Main contributors from the group: Wenjie Luo (topic coordinator), Qun Song, Chaojie Gu, Zhenyu Yan, Duc Van Le, Siyuan Zhou, Jiale Chen, Qiping (Joy) Yang The acoustic and imagery sensing modalities provide abundant information about the sensed target. As a result, the volume of an acoustic/imagery signal sample is often high and the data processing to extract useful information requires intensive computing. Such characteristics introduce various system challenges in data acquisition, computing, storage, and network transmission on networked sensing platforms with constrained processing capabilities, network bandwidth, and bounded energy supply.

PhyAug Physics Directed Data Augmentation for Deep Sensing Model Transfer in Cyber-Physical Systems.
ACM/IEEE IPSN'21 -- Best Artifact Award Runner-Up
Wenjie Luo Zhenyu Yan Qun Song Rui Tan

Domain shift can greatly degrade the performance of deep model. We exploit the first principle governing the domain shift to augment data for retraining the model. In the case studies of speech recognition, with 5-second unlabelled data collected from the target-domain microphones, we can recover the accuracy losses due to microphone characteristic variations by up to 72%.

Resilience Bounds of Network Clock Synchronization with Fault Correction
ACM TOSN'20
Linshan Jiang Rui Tan Arvind Easwaran

Naturally occurring disturbances and malicious attacks can lead to faults in synchronizing the clocks of two network nodes. In this work, we investigate the fundamental resilience bounds of network clock synchronization for a system of N nodes against the peer-to-peer synchronization faults. The analysis in the work provides basic understanding and useful guidelines to the design of resilient clock synchronization systems.

Attack-Aware Data Timestamping in Low-Power Synchronization-Free LoRaWAN
IEEE ICDCS'20
Chaojie Gu Linshan Jiang Rui Tan Mo Li Jun Huang

Maintaining tight clock synchronization of LoRaWAN end devices for data timestamping is costly due to limited bandwidth. We show that timestamping based on uplink frame arrival time at the gateway is efficient but can be insecure. We design a gateway added with the RTL-SDR to analyze the bias of the uplink carrier frequency to improve the security of gateway-side data timestamping.

Frequency modulation Distributed databases Signal processing algorithms Logic gates

Moving Target Defense for Embedded Deep Visual Sensing against Adversarial Examples
ACM SenSys'19
Qun Song Zhenyu Yan Rui Tan

Deep learning-based visual sensing is vulnerable to adversarial examples, which are slightly perturbed inputs to mislead classification models. We follow the moving target defense method to generate multiple new deep models after system deployment, that will collaboratively detect and thwart adversarial examples. It significantly increases the bar of successful attacks. We also apply serial data fusion with early stopping to reduce the inference time.

Neural networks Adversarial examples Moving target defense

Towards Touch-to-Access Device Authentication Using Induced Body Electric Potentials
ACM MobiCom'19
Zhenyu Yan Qun Song Rui Tan Yang Li Adams Wai Kin Kong

We design a device authentication system in which the user wearing a token device can be granted access to touchable IoT objects in indoor environments. Our design exploits the complex time-periodic electric field generated by the powerlines distributed in the indoor space and the coupling between the field and the human body.
